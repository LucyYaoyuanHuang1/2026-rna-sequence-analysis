* When conducting multiple t-tests on many groups, e.g. 20,000+ genes, necessary to control FDR (false discover rate) and reduce Type I Error
* Techniques include Bonferroni Correction and the Benjamini-Hochberg Procedure
o Recommended by Dr. Wang to use BHP
o BHP is less conservative and better for large #s of hypotheses, while Bonferroni Correction is more conservative and better for smaller #s of hypotheses
o BC controls Family-Wise Error Rate (FWER, chance of ANY Type I error across whole family) while BCP controls FDR (False Discovery Rate, Type I error rate in family)

* Beta-Uniform Mixture Model is a statistical method used to model the distribution of unadjusted p-values
o Separates noise and truly significant tests
o Noise is flat/continuous uniform distribution
o Signfigant p-values cluster near 0 and are modeled by a Beta distribution (Beta(a, 1))
o Estimates false positive and negative rate
* Allows for tailored p-value cutoffs
